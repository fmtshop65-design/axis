Tattoo Aftercare: How to Heal a Tattoo Properly

The complete professional aftercare protocol — from the first hour post-session through long-term maintenance — using AXIS Silver Balm.

← Back to Blog

Why Aftercare Determines the Final Result

Tattooing is an open wound procedure. The dermis has been punctured hundreds of thousands of times per session, depositing pigment beneath the epidermis. How well the skin heals directly determines colour vibrancy, line crispness, and long-term tattoo quality.

Poor aftercare causes blowout of healed lines, patchiness in colour, scabbing that pulls out pigment, and increased infection risk. Correct aftercare is the final stage of the artist's craft.

The First 24 Hours

  • Hour 0–4: Keep the tattoo covered with the wrap or film applied by the artist. This protects against environmental contamination while the initial plasma sealing begins.
  • Hour 4–6: Remove the wrap. Gently wash with lukewarm water and a fragrance-free, pH-neutral soap. Do not scrub — use fingertips only in circular motions.
  • After washing: Pat dry with a clean paper towel (not a cloth towel — fibres can carry bacteria). Apply a thin layer of AXIS Silver Balm.

Note: If your artist used a second-skin or hydrocolloid film (Dermalize, Saniderm), follow their specific removal instructions — typically 24–72 hours for the initial application.

Days 1–14: The Healing Protocol

  • Wash 2× daily (morning and evening) with fragrance-free soap and lukewarm water.
  • Apply AXIS Silver Balm after each wash and whenever the tattoo feels tight or dry — typically 2–3 times daily.
  • Avoid: Direct sunlight, UV exposure, swimming, soaking in baths or hot tubs, gym equipment that contacts the tattoo.
  • Do not pick or scratch peeling or flaking skin. The outer layer will shed naturally, carrying no pigment with it if moisturised properly.

AXIS Silver Balm — Active Formulation

AXIS Silver Balm is a pH-balanced, dermatologically tested aftercare cream designed specifically for fresh tattoos. Its formula accelerates cellular regeneration while providing a breathable protective film that prevents scabbing and locks in pigment.

  • Anti-inflammatory agents: Reduce swelling, redness, and skin stress in the first 72 hours.
  • Natural moisturisers: Prevent trans-epidermal water loss, keeping the healing skin supple.
  • Breathable barrier: Protects against environmental contaminants without clogging pores.

Long-Term Care

After the surface is fully healed (typically 3–4 weeks), the dermis continues to settle for up to 3 months. During this period:

  • Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen to the tattoo before any sun exposure — UV is the single biggest cause of premature tattoo fading.
  • Keep the skin moisturised with an unscented lotion.
  • Stay hydrated — skin elasticity and hydration directly affect colour retention.
