AXIS Needle Cartridges: Full Technical Review & Buying Guide

An in-depth look at why AXIS cartridges — designed by professional artists — have become the go-to choice for precision-driven, professional tattooers.

← Back to Blog

In the world of professional tattooing, tool quality directly affects the final result, client skin health, working speed, and even the artist's hand fatigue. Among consumable tools, needle cartridges are one of the most sensitive components. The smallest flaw in build engineering can cause needle wobble, inconsistent ink flow, added skin trauma, and ultimately a drop in work quality.

AXIS needle cartridges — designed and developed by professional Russian tattoo artists and sold in Russia under the name OBJECT — have earned a special place among top-tier artists. That reputation comes from precise engineering, high build standards, and consistent performance: qualities that make it the preferred choice for artists who prioritize control, durability, quiet operation, and smooth needle movement.

In this article, we provide a complete technical review of AXIS needle cartridges: what sets them apart, their core features, their different types, and how to choose the right one for your working style.

What Is AXIS and How Does It Differ From Standard Cartridges?

AXIS cartridges aren't just simple disposable needles — they're an engineered system built for stability, control, and precision. Many low-end cartridges on the market suffer from "needle wobble." This wobble degrades line quality, makes shading harder, causes more skin trauma, and tires the artist's hand faster.

AXIS addresses these issues through a proprietary structure in which:

  • Needle movement is smoother and more linear
  • Pressure is distributed evenly throughout the cartridge body
  • Excess noise and vibration are minimized
  • Ink flows more consistently onto the work surface
  • The artist has greater control over lining, shading, and color packing

This engineering-first approach is exactly what has made AXIS the go-to choice for artists who refuse to compromise on the quality of their work.

Technical Features of AXIS Needle Cartridges

1) 3-Stage Stabilization System

The main advantage of AXIS cartridges is their integrated three-stage stabilization system. This mechanism, positioned at three fixed points inside the housing, holds the needle steady and absorbs unwanted lateral wobble.

Benefits of this structure include:

  • Reduced vibration during machine operation
  • Quieter performance
  • Greater precision on fine lines
  • Reliable stability during long shading sessions
  • Minimal needle deflection

This system is a major advantage for artists who rely on clean, uninterrupted linework.

2) 316L Surgical Stainless Steel Needles

AXIS needles are made from 316L medical-grade stainless steel. Known for high corrosion resistance, structural strength, and biocompatibility, this alloy is the gold standard in medical and tattoo applications.

Key benefits of 316L steel:

  • High resistance to rust and wear
  • Maintains structural integrity throughout the session
  • Fully compatible with sterilization processes
  • Reduced risk of tip dulling

In addition, AXIS uses laser technology to check the sharpening angle of every needle tip, ensuring consistent skin penetration and clean cutting.

3) Durable, Hypoallergenic Plastic Housing

The outer shell of AXIS cartridges is made from medical-grade, hypoallergenic plastic. Though it may seem like a simple choice, it directly affects hygiene, performance, and comfort.

Benefits of this housing include:

  • Reduced risk of skin irritation or allergic reaction
  • High resistance to pressure and machine-induced stress
  • Lightweight design for better hand balance
  • Higher hygiene and safety standards

A housing that's both durable and lightweight helps reduce hand fatigue during long working hours.

4) Ergonomic Capillary Tip Design

One of AXIS's standout features is its Capillary Tip geometry. This design improves ink fluid dynamics and ensures a steady, controlled flow from the reservoir to the needle tip.

Benefits of this design:

  • Smoother, more predictable ink flow
  • Fewer interruptions for ink reloading
  • Even pigment saturation across the needles
  • Easier cleaning during color changes thanks to smooth interior walls
  • Superior control during shading and color packing

5) High-Endurance Internal Membrane

The safety membrane is vital for protecting the machine, acting as a barrier that prevents ink and bodily fluids from flowing back into the grip and motor. AXIS uses a membrane with medium tension and high wear resistance.

This component provides:

  • Enhanced protection for expensive rotary machines
  • Complete prevention of fluid back-flow
  • Long-term retention of membrane elasticity
  • Smooth, balanced needle return

Types of AXIS Cartridges

AXIS is available in several configurations to match different techniques:

Round Liner (RL)

Needles soldered tightly in a circular formation, ideal for lining and detail work. Best for:

  • Fine lines and clean contours
  • Lettering and script
  • Micro-realism and dotwork

Round Shader (RS)

A circular grouping with wider needle spacing. Suited for small-area shading and color packing in tight spaces. Best for:

  • Soft shading and gradients
  • Filling small areas
  • Building texture

Magnum (M1)

Two rows of needles in a flat, woven configuration. The classic choice for broad coverage. Best for:

  • Solid color packing
  • Heavy blackwork
  • Soft color gradients

Round Magnum (RM)

Also known as Curved Magnum, this configuration has a curved edge that follows the natural motion of the hand, reducing edge trauma to the skin. Best for:

  • Smooth, seamless shading
  • Subtle color blending
  • Realistic textures

Needle Diameter (Gauge) and Taper in AXIS

To match your working style, AXIS offers three main diameters:

  • 0.25mm (Ultra-fine): The best option for micro-detail, very thin lines, and delicate dotwork.
  • 0.30mm (Medium): The most versatile and balanced choice for everyday lining and soft shading.
  • 0.35mm (Standard): Great for bold lines, heavy color packing, and solid blackwork.

Long Taper vs. Extra Long Taper

  • Long Taper: Offers good ink flow and strong control, suited for general tattooing.
  • Extra Long Taper: Provides a sharper tip for maximum precision and reduces skin trauma — ideal for intricate detail and clean lining.

Pro tip: Choosing the right taper depends on your machine's stroke length, your hand speed, and your client's skin density. Try different configurations to find your ideal setup.

Why Professional Artists Choose AXIS

AXIS cartridges are built for professionals who need consistency. They deliver:

  • Uncompromising precision: Clean lines and predictable ink deposition.
  • Reduced hand fatigue: Less vibration means a more comfortable grip during long sessions.
  • Longevity: Needles hold their sharpness, and the membrane retains its tension throughout the session.
  • Excellent ink management: The Capillary Tip reduces ink splatter and provides smoother flow.

Quick Comparison: AXIS vs. Standard Cartridges

  • Stability: Standard cartridges often suffer from lateral needle wobble; AXIS uses a 3-stage stabilization system for linear movement.
  • Needle quality: Standard options may have inconsistent sharpness; AXIS uses 316L steel with laser-checked precision.
  • Ink flow: Standard tips often splatter or pool ink; AXIS features a capillary design for controlled flow.
  • Safety: AXIS has a medical-grade housing and high-tension membrane, unlike generic cartridges with unreliable membranes.

Usage Guide and Best Practices

  • Always make sure your rotary machine runs at the correct voltage for the cartridge configuration.
  • Inspect the needle tip under magnification before starting a session.
  • Don't force the needle — let the stabilization system and machine stroke do the work.
  • Dispose of the cartridge in a sharps container immediately after use.
